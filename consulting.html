Build consensus around diagrams not meetings

Treasure mapping can be used to build consensus around your embedded system or full stack web application.  Richard Burd will review your code then hold 1 on 1 zoom meetings with each of your engineers individually and figure out where they fail to see eye-to-eye with each other.  Richard will then render all of the variables in a single treasure map that will be shared with everyone on your design team so each member has a bird's eye view of systems and sub-systems affecting their work.  This process ensures that each team member understands the layered nuance and abstractions they couldn't see in the various slack channels & email chains passed around the team.

Your software is your investment

Don't rely on spoken language to onboard new developers because it is no longer a sufficient medium for transferring information from one human brain to another.  Our technologies have simply grown too complex for language alone to explain how things work.  Today we need intuitive, visually engaging illustrations that show how a build works along with concise language that gets right to the point when we are looking at a particular function or sub-system.  When you outsource, sell, or change up team members, you want your intellectual property documented in a way that is understandable to a wide audience.  Your documentation should be an asset people look at to become more productive, not an afterthought for your organization.

Building internal reference guides

Developers hate documentation because it is boring and doesn't answer the question they're looking for fast enough.  Richard Burd uses dense information overlapping to give you the best viewing experience possible informed by cutting edge pedagogical & neuroscientific theories of how your brain manages information.  All to often your developers are busy trying to remember details they are better off remembering where to find instead.  Our brains have limited RAM that is best managed rather than overloaded.
